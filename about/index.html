About Me

I'm Clay Ewing, a game designer, creative technologist, and educator dedicated to leveraging interactive media for social impact and meaningful change. My career has been built around a passion for creating experiences that engage, educate, and inspire action. Whether through serious games, educational platforms, or immersive storytelling, I believe in the power of interactive design to make complex issues relatable and drive positive outcomes.

Throughout my work, I have focused on bridging the gap between technology and societal needs. I lead NERDLab, an academic game studio at the University of Miami, where students and I collaborate on social impact projects. Our projects have been recognized with numerous awards and featured at prestigious conferences and exhibitions worldwide.

I am passionate about mentoring new talent and fostering collaborative environments. Through NERDLab, I encourage students to explore their unique talents—whether they’re artists new to digital design, writers delving into interactive storytelling, or coders venturing into game engines for the first time. My goal is to guide them as they turn their ideas into tangible products, reinforcing the belief that everyone has the potential to contribute meaningfully to the creative process.

From the academic world to community outreach, my journey is rooted in curiosity, innovation, and a drive for positive change. I welcome collaboration with researchers, educators, and creators who share a vision of harnessing the potential of interactive media to make a difference.

Let’s connect and create something impactful.
